Pretec Commerce Platform
Platform Integrations — Customer Overview
A plain-language description of how the different systems in the Pretec webshop platform connect and work together.
Audience: Pretec Prepared by: Geta Date: June 2026

The Pretec webshop is not a single system — it is made up of several specialised tools that each do one job well. To make them work as one platform, data needs to flow between them automatically.

This document describes, in plain terms, each of the six connections ("integrations") that make the platform work. Decisions have been agreed for all six; the only remaining open item is providing RamBase API credentials so Geta can verify the final technical details of the live-price and quote connections.

The six integrations
1
Products & Categories into the Content System
From RamBase (ERP) → Struct (product content system)
RamBase → Harmony → Struct — automatic, every 10 minutes

Whenever a product is created or changed in RamBase (your ERP system), that product automatically appears in Struct — the tool where Pretec adds photos, descriptions, technical details, and other content that makes the product look good on the webshop.

RamBase stays in charge of the core product information (product number, name, status, unit of measure). Struct is where your team enriches it with everything a customer needs to make a purchase decision. The two are kept in sync automatically every 10 minutes, so your content team never needs to manually re-enter product data.

The same applies to product categories: the category structure from RamBase is brought into Struct every 10 minutes, where it can be enriched with images and descriptions for the webshop navigation.

✓ Agreed
  • Sync frequency: every 10 minutes
  • When a product is deactivated in RamBase, it is automatically removed from listings on the webshop. Its product page URL remains accessible, showing "Ikke lenger tilgjengelig" (No longer available) — so existing bookmarks and external links are not broken.
2
Publishing Products to the Webshop
From Struct (product content system) → Storefront (the webshop catalogue)
Struct → Harmony → Webshop catalogue — automatic, every 10 minutes

Once a product is enriched in Struct — description written, images added — Pretec's content team marks it as "Published for web". From that moment, this integration takes care of making it visible on the webshop automatically within 10 minutes.

This is the second half of the product journey: integration 1 brings products in to Struct for enrichment, and integration 2 sends them out to the webshop once they are ready. Products that have not been marked as ready never appear on the webshop, regardless of their status in RamBase — this gives Pretec full control over the launch timing of new products.

✓ Agreed
  • A product only appears on the webshop when a Pretec editor explicitly marks it "Published for web" in Struct — no automatic publishing.
  • When a product is deactivated in RamBase or unpublished in Struct, it is removed from all listings and search results. Its product page URL remains accessible, showing "Ikke lenger tilgjengelig".
  • Sync frequency: every 10 minutes.
3
Stock & Availability on the Webshop
From RamBase (ERP) → Storefront (the webshop catalogue)
RamBase → Harmony → Webshop catalogue — every 10 minutes

This integration keeps the webshop up to date with what is actually in stock. Stock levels from RamBase are sent to the webshop every 10 minutes, so logged-in customers can see whether a product is available before placing an order.

Availability is shown as a simple "På lager" (In stock) or "Ikke på lager" (Out of stock) indicator — no exact numbers are displayed. An out-of-stock item can still be added to the cart and submitted as a quote request; Pretec Sales handles availability and fulfilment from RamBase.

✓ Agreed
  • Display: "På lager" / "Ikke på lager" — no exact quantities shown.
  • Availability is only visible to logged-in customers. Anonymous visitors do not see stock status.
  • Sync frequency: every 10 minutes. Stock changes in RamBase appear on the webshop within approximately 10 minutes.
  • The exact RamBase stock field and warehouse scope will be confirmed during integration setup.
4
Customer Accounts & New Company Registration
RamBase (ERP) ↔ Webshop — both directions
RamBase → Webshop — existing customers, every 10 minutes
Webshop → RamBase — new companies, on approval

This is the only integration that flows in both directions. It covers two separate situations:

Existing customers: Companies and contact persons that already exist in RamBase are automatically brought into the webshop every 10 minutes. When an existing customer logs in for the first time, their company information is already there — no manual setup needed.

New company applications: When a new company applies for access to the webshop, Pretec Sales reviews and approves the application in the Maestro back-office. On approval, the company is automatically created in RamBase — so the Pretec team does not have to create it twice. Before approving, Sales fills in three fields: shipment arrangement, shipment partner customer number (if applicable), and CoBuilder membership. The RamBase customer ID is then linked back to the webshop account automatically.

If an applicant provides an organisation number that already exists in RamBase, the application is blocked and the applicant is directed to contact Pretec Sales directly. This prevents duplicate company records.

✓ Agreed
  • Duplicate check: organisation number matched against existing RamBase companies. Duplicate → application blocked, applicant directed to contact Sales.
  • Approval fields Pretec Sales fills in: shipment arrangement, shipment partner customer number, CoBuilder membership.
  • When a customer is deactivated in RamBase, their webshop access is blocked within 10 minutes (next sync cycle).
5
Editorial Content on the Webshop
From Sanity (content management) → Storefront (the webshop)
Sanity CMS → Webshop — live on publish

Not everything on the webshop is a product. Pages like About, Contact, Terms and conditions, and similar informational pages need to be written and maintained by Pretec's team. Sanity is the tool where this editorial content is authored.

When content is published in Sanity, it automatically appears on the webshop within seconds — no developer needed. This gives Pretec full control over the non-product content on the site.

The webshop navigation (the menu structure) is driven automatically from the product category tree, not from Sanity. Pretec does not need to manually maintain the navigation — it updates automatically as categories change in RamBase.

✓ Agreed
  • Phase 1 content type: editorial landing pages only (About, Contact, Terms, etc.). Category banners and campaign banners are Phase 2.
  • Navigation is driven automatically from the product category tree — no authoring in Sanity required.
  • Content publishes live to the webshop within seconds of being published in Sanity.
Still to confirm with Pretec: Which specific editorial pages are needed at launch, and who at Pretec will have authoring rights in Sanity Studio.
6
Secure Connection to RamBase
Foundation for all RamBase-connected services (price, cart, orders, customer sync)

Integrations 1, 3, and 4 all read from or write to RamBase, as do the live price lookup and order history features of the webshop. This integration describes the secure connection layer that all of them share — how the platform authenticates to RamBase, how credentials are stored safely, and how the connection is managed across different environments (test, staging, and production).

From Pretec's perspective, the main requirement is straightforward: Pretec provides the API access to RamBase with the right permissions, and Geta takes care of the secure setup and ongoing operation. Credentials are stored in a secure vault and can be rotated without downtime. Separate credentials are used for the test and production environments.

Waiting on Pretec — RamBase API credentials: Access to the RamBase test environment is needed before Geta can confirm six outstanding technical details — including how live prices are looked up per customer, exact order filter fields, and how new sales quotes are created. All other decisions for this integration are resolved. Once credentials are available, these items can be verified quickly.
