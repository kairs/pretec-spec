Pretec Commerce Platform
Platform Integrations — Customer Overview
A plain-language description of how the different systems in the Pretec webshop platform connect and work together.
Audience: Pretec Prepared by: Geta Date: June 2026

The Pretec webshop is not a single system — it is made up of several specialised tools that each do one job well. To make them work as one platform, data needs to flow between them automatically.

This document describes, in plain terms, each of the six connections ("integrations") that make the platform work. For each one you will find: what it does, why it matters, and what still needs to be agreed before it can be built.

The six integrations
1
Products & Categories into the Content System
From RamBase (ERP) → Struct (product content system)
RamBase → Harmony → Struct — automatic, scheduled

Whenever a product is created or changed in RamBase (your ERP system), that product automatically appears in Struct — the tool where Pretec adds photos, descriptions, technical details, and other content that makes the product look good on the webshop.

RamBase stays in charge of the core product information (product number, name, status, unit of measure). Struct is where your team enriches it with everything a customer needs to make a purchase decision. The two are kept in sync automatically, so your content team never needs to manually re-enter product data.

The same applies to product categories: the category structure from RamBase is brought into Struct, where it can be enriched with images and descriptions for the webshop navigation.

Still to agree with Pretec: How often should products sync — hourly, nightly, or as soon as a change happens in RamBase? What should happen in the webshop when a product is deactivated in RamBase?
2
Publishing Products to the Webshop
From Struct (product content system) → Storefront (the webshop catalogue)
Struct → Harmony → Webshop catalogue — automatic

Once a product is enriched in Struct — description written, images added — this integration takes care of making it visible in the webshop. It reads the finished product from Struct and publishes it to the webshop catalogue automatically.

This is the second half of the product journey: integration 1 brings products in to Struct, and integration 2 sends them out to the webshop. When a product is unpublished or deactivated, the webshop is updated to match.

Still to agree with Pretec: What makes a product "ready to publish" — is there a specific flag or checklist in Struct? What should the webshop show when a product is no longer available?
3
Stock & Availability on the Webshop
From RamBase (ERP) → Storefront (the webshop catalogue)
RamBase → Harmony → Webshop catalogue — regular updates

This integration keeps the webshop up to date with what is actually in stock. Stock levels from RamBase are sent to the webshop on a regular schedule, so customers can see whether a product is available, low in stock, or out of stock before they place an order.

The plan for Phase 1 is to update availability at regular intervals (for example every 15 minutes or every hour), rather than in real time. This is a practical starting point — the exact frequency and how availability is shown (exact numbers or simple bands like "in stock / out of stock") still needs to be agreed.

Still to agree with Pretec: Should the webshop show exact stock numbers or just "in stock / low / out of stock"? How quickly must stock changes appear on the webshop? Should anonymous visitors see availability, or only logged-in customers?
4
Customer Accounts & New Company Registration
RamBase (ERP) ↔ Webshop — both directions
RamBase → Webshop — existing customers, on a schedule
Webshop → RamBase — new companies, on approval

This is the only integration that flows in both directions. It covers two separate situations:

Existing customers: Companies and contact persons that already exist in RamBase are automatically brought into the webshop on a schedule. When an existing customer logs in for the first time, their company information is already there — no manual setup needed.

New company applications: When a new company applies for access to the webshop, Pretec Sales reviews and approves the application in the Maestro back-office. On approval, the company is automatically created in RamBase as well — so the Pretec team does not have to create it twice. The RamBase customer ID is linked back to the webshop account automatically.

Still to agree with Pretec: If an applicant names a company that already exists in RamBase, should the webshop link to the existing record or create a new one? What information does Pretec Sales fill in when approving a new company that goes into RamBase?
5
Editorial Content on the Webshop
From Sanity (content management) → Storefront (the webshop)
Sanity CMS → Webshop — on publish

Not everything on the webshop is a product. Pages like the front page, campaign banners, category descriptions, and the navigation menu need to be written and maintained by Pretec's team. Sanity is the tool where this editorial content is authored.

When content is published in Sanity, it automatically appears on the webshop — no developer needed. This gives Pretec full control over the non-product content: what to feature, what campaigns to run, and how the site is structured.

On pages like category pages, the editorial content from Sanity and the product catalogue work together — Pretec writes the category banner and description in Sanity, and the products appear alongside it from the catalogue automatically.

Still to agree with Pretec: Which types of content are needed for Phase 1 (landing pages, category descriptions, banners, navigation)? Who in the Pretec team will be authoring and approving content in Sanity?
6
Secure Connection to RamBase
Foundation for all RamBase-connected services (price, cart, orders, customer sync)

Integrations 1, 3, and 4 all read from or write to RamBase, as do the price lookup and order history features of the webshop. This integration describes the secure connection layer that all of them share — how the platform authenticates to RamBase, how credentials are stored safely, and how the connection is managed across different environments (test, staging, and production).

From Pretec's perspective, the main requirement is straightforward: Pretec provides the API access to RamBase with the right permissions, and Geta takes care of the secure setup and ongoing operation. Credentials are stored in a secure vault and can be rotated without downtime.

Waiting on Pretec: Access to the RamBase test environment is needed before Geta can confirm several open technical details — including how live prices are looked up per customer and how new sales quotes are created. These items are noted and will be resolved once credentials are available.
Summary of open items

The following questions need to be answered by Pretec before the integrations above can be fully specified and built. They have been raised individually in each section above and are collected here for convenience.

Products (1 & 2): Sync frequency. What marks a product as ready to publish. What the webshop shows when a product is deactivated.

Stock (3): Acceptable staleness of stock data. Whether to show exact quantities or stock bands. Whether anonymous visitors see availability.

Customers (4): De-duplication rule for company applications. Extra information Pretec Sales fills in when approving a new company. What happens when a customer is deactivated in RamBase.

Content (5): Which content types are in Phase 1 scope. Who authors and approves content in Sanity.

RamBase access (6): Provide API credentials for the test environment so Geta can verify the remaining open technical points.
